Causes of Depression

Depression can be caused by a combination of biological, genetic, environmental, and psychological factors. Some common causes include:

  • Family history of depression
  • Chemical imbalances in the brain
  • Chronic illness or pain
  • Stressful life events
  • Alcohol or drug abuse
  • Lack of social support

Symptoms of Depression

Depression can cause a variety of physical and emotional symptoms, including:

  • Feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or helplessness
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
  • Loss of energy or fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Physical symptoms such as headaches or stomachaches
  • Thoughts of suicide or self-harm

Treatment for Depression

Depression can be treated with a combination of medication, psychotherapy, and lifestyle changes. Some common treatments include:

  • Antidepressant medication
  • Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Interpersonal therapy (IPT)
  • Exercise and physical activity
  • Healthy eating habits
  • Social support and community involvement
